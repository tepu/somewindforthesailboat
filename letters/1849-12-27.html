Drimure 27 Dec. 1849 In Howard Township

My Dear Child,

I take this opportunity of letting you know that we are all in tolerable good health at present thanks be to the Almighty for all his mercies to us. I am suffering very much by the sore that is on my leg, but your Sister [Elizabeth] and family are all in good health. I hope you will not delay in letting me have an answer to this as soon as you can, and let me know what that 10£ make in your money in that Country, as it is most likely that it may be the last you will get from me, my age and this sore that is on me may take me away sooner than expected.

I hope you will make good use of what I now send you.

Your sister and family joins me in their best wishes to you and Family.

I remain yours affectionate
Mother Ann Scott

The address on this letter
Mrs. Michael Ogletree
Township of Howard
Western District
British North America
– Ochililla
