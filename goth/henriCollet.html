The Amazing Conversations of Mssr. Henri Collet

"Oh, you really must stay for dinner" the Marchioness Leduc insisted "We're having Henri Collet over, I've told you about him. That absolutely waggish chap I met shooting in Nantes."

"I can't say I recall" I replied, overjoyed to have a reason to stay as the Marchioness had so cordially offered me the chaise closest to the drink cart. Whatever I may have felt regarding being on my third Bordeaux since luncheon evaporated remembering she was on her third Anatolian tan of the year.

"One aspect of Henri's character that I can't stress enough, indeed the only aspect worth mentioning is the man's sheer talent for understatement. His capacity for it is unbounded, it makes him absolutely drôle."

That evening after we had dressed for dinner, but before the repas itself, I had retired for a brief Turkish cigarette and a volume of Defoe with which I was becoming only just acquainted when the footman presented Henri to the Marchioness and myself.

"This is certainly a little hill you live on" Henri said to the Marchioness, who burst into uproarious laughter at this.

"Did I not tell you William that this man has an unbridled taste for understatement?" the Marchioness said smiling, "this is the tallest mountain in the country!"

Henri continued "I had a spot of the malbec you're so fond of, it was every bit as divine as you promised"

"A spot!?" shouted the Marquis, still dressed from riding, coming up the hall, "you've seen the bottom of at least two bottles today. William, doesn't Henri have a fabulous talent for understatement?"

Henri took this compliment jovially. "You should send your valet de pied to my carriage, there is a bottle of cabernet franc in my valise I think we should open now that the four of us are all together. You know William, I have a few vines around my château."

"A few vines! My word" exclaimed the Marchioness "back at it again, indefatigable. Mssr. Collet, I know for a fact you have nearly two thousand acres under the plow."

"I've seen it myself!" the Marquis added "Nine different terroirs, and he calls it 'a few vines'. You are truly the Molière of understating a situation, Henri."

"I understand you were here for tiffin, William, how did you find the tuck?" the Marquis asked. The Marquis was a big game man, and an army man before that. In lieu of spectacles, he saw the world through the tasty prism of dinner. A slice of ham was the only corrective lens he ever used. Getting a feel for the sport, I tried my hand at understatement.

"I had but a single grain of rice." I said, smiling.

The silence was immediate. The Marchioness looked aghast, the Marquis furious.

"God, Bill, you've really shit the bed this time, haven't you."
